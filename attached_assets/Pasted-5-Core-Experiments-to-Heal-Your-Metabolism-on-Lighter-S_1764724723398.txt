üö´ Experiment 3: Eliminate PUFAs (Polyunsaturated Fats)
What it is:
Systematically remove seed oils and PUFA-rich foods from your diet and replace them with stable, metabolism-supporting fats.
How to do it:
Foods/oils to eliminate:

Canola, soybean, corn, sunflower, safflower oils
Vegetable oil and "blended" oils
Most restaurant and packaged foods (read labels carefully)
Margarine and industrial spreads
Most nuts and seeds (especially in large quantities)
Conventional chicken and pork (high in PUFA from feed)

Replace with:

Butter (grass-fed when possible)
Coconut oil
Olive oil (in moderation, not heated)
Beef tallow or lamb fat
Dairy fats (cream, cheese, whole milk)
Cacao butter

Why it matters:
PUFAs are chemically unstable and easily oxidize in your body, where they accumulate in cell membranes and interfere with mitochondrial respiration. They directly suppress thyroid function, block glucose metabolism, promote inflammation, and increase estrogen dominance. Removing them is often the single most powerful metabolic intervention you can make.
What you might notice:

Increased body warmth (especially in hands and feet)
More stable energy without crashes
Reduced inflammation and pain
Better hormone balance
Improved sleep quality
Weight loss or easier weight management
Clearer thinking

The healing insight:
PUFAs have accumulated in your tissues over years or decades. As you stop consuming them and they gradually clear from your system, your mitochondria can finally produce energy efficiently again. This experiment often creates the most dramatic and sustained improvements because you're removing a fundamental metabolic blocker.
Timeline note: Full PUFA clearance can take 2‚Äì4 years, but most people notice significant improvements within weeks to months.
