What Stress Really Is — And Why It Steals Your Energy

In the bioenergetic (pro-metabolic) view, “stress” isn’t just a feeling.
It’s a physiological state your body enters when it senses:

low energy

danger

undernourishment

cold

overexertion

emotional overwhelm

chronic pressure

In this state, your body shifts from energy production → to energy survival.

That switch is powered by stress hormones—mainly cortisol and adrenaline—and over time, they slow your metabolism, increase inflammation, disrupt digestion, and make weight loss harder.

This is why stress isn’t just “in your head.”
It’s in your cells.

How Stress Slows Your Metabolism

When your body can’t make enough energy easily (low thyroid, low glucose availability, poor sleep, skipping meals), it turns to the stress system.

Here’s what happens:

1. Cortisol rises to maintain blood sugar

Cortisol breaks down your muscle and tissues into amino acids and converts them into glucose (a process called gluconeogenesis).
This keeps you alive — but it’s expensive.

Over time, high cortisol leads to:

slower metabolism

more fat storage (especially belly)

cravings

mood swings

sleep issues

feeling wired but tired

2. Adrenaline keeps you going when you should be resting

Adrenaline gives short bursts of energy, but long-term it causes:

anxiety

heart palpitations

cold hands and feet

shaky hunger

digestive shutdown

3. Thyroid function drops

Stress hormones directly suppress thyroid output — meaning:

lower body temp

lower pulse

lower energy

slower digestion

slower fat burning

This is why so many people think they have a willpower problem—
it’s actually a low-energy problem.

The Pro-Metabolic Approach to Lowering Stress

Instead of pushing through stress —
the bioenergetic view focuses on giving the body energy so it no longer needs the stress response.

This is why Lighter™ teaches:

1. Eat enough & eat regularly

Stable blood sugar = lower cortisol.

2. Prioritize carbs + protein together

Fruit + dairy + gelatin + roots = stable energy.

3. Warmth and safety signals

Warm environments help shift you out of stress mode.

4. Gentle movement (not punishing workouts)

Walking, restorative lifting, light movement — keeps energy steady.

5. Sunlight & circadian rhythm

Light lowers stress hormones and boosts metabolism.

6. Support digestion

A calm gut signals safety to the nervous system.

7. Avoid PUFAs (polyunsaturated fats)

These fats increase inflammation and stress load.

Why We Track Temperature & Pulse

These two numbers tell you instantly:

Am I in stress mode or energy mode?

Low temperature + low pulse → low metabolism, high stress load

Normal temperature + normal pulse → body feels safe

High pulse + low temp → adrenaline-driven stress

Low pulse + high temp → thyroid not keeping up

This is why we start with these simple vitals.

They cut through confusion and tell you the truth about what’s happening in your cells — in real time.
